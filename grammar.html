Advanced IELTS Grammar Tips

Achieve Band 7.5+ with these grammar rules and strategies

1. Master Complex Sentences

Why it’s crucial: High-band IELTS responses demonstrate a range of sentence structures, especially complex and compound-complex sentences. They showcase your ability to express nuanced ideas.

How to practice:

  • Use subordinating conjunctions like although, whereas, despite, and while.
  • Combine clauses to show relationships between ideas, e.g.: “Although many people prefer cars for convenience, using public transport significantly reduces environmental impact.”
“Good grammar is credibility, especially on the Internet.” — Amanda Armstrong

2. Advanced Tenses

Why it’s crucial: Show a range of verb tenses, especially in Writing Task 1 and Speaking.

How to practice:

  • Learn to describe trends using perfect and perfect continuous tenses:
    • “The number of users has increased steadily over the past decade.”
    • “The population had been growing until 2015 when it began to decline.”
  • Use modal verbs to express probability or suggestions:
    • “Governments should implement stricter policies to address climate change.”

3. Varied Sentence Beginnings

Why it’s crucial: Avoid repetitive sentence structures, which can sound monotonous and limit your score.

How to practice:

  • Start sentences with different elements:
    • Adverbs: Interestingly, most people disagree with this view.
    • Participial phrases: Having completed the study, researchers concluded...
    • Conjunctions or transitions: Moreover, this approach is cost-effective.

4. Precision in Prepositions

Why it’s crucial: Misused prepositions are a common issue for IELTS candidates.

How to practice:

  • Familiarize yourself with prepositions in common collocations, such as:
    • “Interested in,” “capable of,” “concerned about.”
  • Practice Writing Task 1 reports to perfect prepositions for graphs and maps, e.g.:
    • “The number increased by 20%.”
    • “The restaurant is located in the south of the city.”

5. Vocabulary and Grammar Cohesion

Why it’s crucial: Cohesion involves linking words (like however, furthermore) and grammatical patterns that tie ideas together.

How to practice:

  • Use cohesive devices effectively but sparingly. Overuse can lower clarity.
  • Practice embedding relative clauses: “The book, which was written in 1920, is still widely studied today.”
